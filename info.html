What is Climate Change?

Climate change is not just "global warming." It is the climates of the world becoming more and more extreme. Climate change is here and caused by humans.
It is caused by greenhouse gasses, things like carbon dioxide and methane. These gasses come from us burning fossil fuels, and are also made from manufacturing the various things we enjoy on a day-to-day basis.
You've probably already heard this about a million times before, but why should you care?

Droughts and Fires

Climate change is causing larger heatwaves and droughts, especially in the southwestern United States. They are predicted to get even worse in the coming years.
These effects could have played a part in California's recent drought, and the California wildfires that have destroyed many homes, communities, and lives.

Storms and Hurricanes

The amount of rain certain areas get have become greatly skewed. Some areas are getting unusual amounts of rain, while others get a lot less. Storms are getting worse.
In recent years, hurricanes have become stronger and more violent. You don't need to be a climate scientist to understand this. You only need to look at the destruction caused by the hurricanes in the eastern United States.

Flooding

While unusually high amounts of rain has contributed to flooding, there is something much worse that could happen due to climate change.
Over the next several decades, the sea level could rise a few feet. While this may not seem like much, this will flood many cities, especially the coastal ones. Coastal cities hold a large portion of the population, so flooding on a global scale would be absolutely devastating.
The effects of climate change are already here, they are already hurting us, and will only get worse. However, we still have time. If we continue on our current path, about 2030 is the point of no return.
But even small actions can buy us a little more time.
